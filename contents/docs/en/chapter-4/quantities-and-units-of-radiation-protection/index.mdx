---
title: Quantities and Units of Radiation Protection
description: Equivalent dose, effective dose, and the sievert as radiation protection quantities.
---

In radiation protection, specific quantities are used to describe the biological impact of ionizing radiation. The most basic quantity, the absorbed dose, does not fully describe the biological effect, because different types of radiation can produce different levels of damage for the same amount of deposited energy.

To account for this, the concept of equivalent dose is used. Equivalent dose takes into consideration the type of radiation, since some types (such as alpha particles) are more biologically damaging than others (such as X-rays or gamma rays). It is calculated by multiplying the absorbed dose by a radiation weighting factor, and its unit is the sievert (Sv).

In addition, different tissues and organs in the body have different sensitivities to radiation. To reflect this, the concept of effective dose is used. Effective dose combines the equivalent doses received by various tissues, each weighted according to its radiosensitivity. It provides a single value that represents the overall risk to the whole body and is also expressed in sieverts (Sv) (Baeyens et al., 2023; IAEA, 2010).
