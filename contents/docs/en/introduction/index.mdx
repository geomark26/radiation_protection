---
title: "Introduction"
description: "Overview of this educational application on radiation protection for non-radiation healthcare workers."
---

The use of ionizing radiation (X-rays, gamma rays, and particulate radiation) in medical applications represents a significant and steadily increasing source of radiation exposure worldwide. Adequate education and training in radiation protection are legally mandated for occupationally exposed workers and are essential for ensuring safety and regulatory compliance.

Over the past decade, asynchronous e-learning programs have been increasingly adopted in professional education. Their flexibility and accessibility make them valuable tools, particularly for healthcare workers with demanding schedules. Research has demonstrated that such programs are at least as effective as traditional instructional methods in improving knowledge and competence among healthcare personnel (Aryee et al., 2024; Charmchi et al., 2025; Panda & Parashar, 2022; Sinclair et al., 2016).

This educational application has been developed for non-radiation healthcare workers, such as ward nurses, emergency department and operating room staff, paramedics, and auxiliary personnel, who are not typically required to undergo radiation protection education and training, as they are not expected to be occupationally exposed to ionizing radiation. However, under certain circumstances, exposure may occur, for example, during the use of portable X-ray units in emergency departments or intensive care units, during the urgent care of patients who have been administered radiopharmaceuticals, in the absence of patient caregivers (comforters and carers), or as a result of accidental exposure.

In such situations, on-site guidance is generally provided by a professional trained in radiation protection. Nevertheless, prior education in the basic principles of radiation protection is beneficial, as it enhances both individual safety and the overall effectiveness of healthcare delivery (European Commission, 2014; International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA], 2005; International Commission on Radiological Protection [ICRP], 2013, 2019).

The education and training of personnel who are not directly involved in the use of ionizing radiation, but who support practices involving such radiation, are required under applicable radiation protection regulations. The responsibility for ensuring the provision of such education and training lies with the organization responsible for the practice. Training may be provided internally by the designated medical physicist at the commencement of employment in the relevant field and should be repeated at regular intervals or whenever deemed necessary. This application may also serve as a refresher tool, reinforcing the fundamental principles of radiation protection among this category of personnel (Council of the European Union, 2013; Joint Ministerial Decision 45872/2019, 2019; Joint Ministerial Decision 32083/2022, 2022; Presidential Decree 101/2018, 2018).

The contents of this educational application is based on the guidelines of the Greek Atomic Energy Commission (EEAE, 2023) for the education of occupationally exposed workers (non-medical personnel), adapted to meet the objectives of this application. The material is organized into five modules, each of which can be studied independently within a short time frame and is followed by a corresponding self-assessment test. Additionally, a comprehensive self-assessment test combining questions from all five modules is available. The tests draw questions randomly from a question bank, ensuring variability and reinforcement of learning. The application also provides the option of language selection between English and Greek, thereby enhancing accessibility for a broader audience.
